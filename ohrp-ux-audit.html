Top opportunities
Audience-first entry page
Split homepage into three clear lanes: Researchers/Investigators, IRB Staff, and Public. Each lane surfaces the 3–5 most-needed tasks immediately.
Content freshness signals
Add prominent “Last verified” badges to all regulatory pages, ideally with a link to what changed. A 2020 review date on a regulations page is a compliance risk for users.
Decision chart disambiguation
Replace the two ambiguous chart sets with a single entry that asks “When did your research begin?” and routes accordingly. Kill the cognitive fork on entry.
Streamline left-nav depth
Collapse the 3-level deep Regulations nav to a max of 2 levels. Move archived/marginal content (pre-2018 charts, archived materials) behind an “Older resources” disclosure.
Prioritized recommendations
  • 1.Fix content staleness: Add an automated “last reviewed” alert banner on any page whose review date is older than 18 months. This is a zero-cost trust repair.
  • 2.Restructure the homepage around audience intent, not org structure. The current 8-card layout mirrors OHRP's internal divisions, not user mental models.
  • 3.Reduce left-nav depth on interior pages. The Regulations section's 3-level tree loads on every page — users cannot form a stable mental map. Cap at 2 levels; put depth behind expandable sections.
  • 4.Surface a prominent “Which decision chart applies to my study?” disambiguation tool where both chart links currently live.
  • 5.Audit and remove or demote zombie pages (Get a Web Button, older archived correspondence lists) that dilute navigation signal.
  • 6.Add plain-language participant entry in the header alongside researcher/IRB links — currently the public has no wayfinding signal on arrival.
